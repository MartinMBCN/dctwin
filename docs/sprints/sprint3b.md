Overview Brief
The Overview Brief is an evidence-based synthesis of the current Digital Career Twin, intended to increase the user's professional self-awareness by surfacing every professionally significant characteristic supported by the available evidence.

Principles
1. Discernment
The Overview Brief should distinguish clearly between observation and interpretation.
Observations are objective characteristics directly supported by evidence.
Interpretations are higher-order patterns supported by multiple observations.
The briefing should establish the observations before drawing interpretations.
Every interpretation should be traceable to multiple observations.

3. Professional Salience
The Overview Brief should surface characteristics that are likely to influence professional understanding of the individual.
These include:
* Recurring themes.
* Career progression.
* Significant transitions.
* Distinctive characteristics.
* Unusual patterns.
* Missing or weakly supported areas.
* Observations likely to attract attention during recruitment, promotion or executive assessment.
The briefing should not attempt to report every available fact. It should report every professionally significant fact.

3. Negative Evidence
The briefing should not avoid observations simply because they may be perceived negatively.
Career gaps, frequent role changes, domain transitions, lack of management experience, or limited evidence supporting a particular capability should be reported when professionally significant.
Such observations should be presented neutrally, without speculation or judgement.

4. Evidence Priority
The prominence of observations within the Overview Brief should reflect the strength, recurrence and quality of the supporting evidence. Briefings should not over-emphasize isolated or weakly supported themes.

5.Professional Proportionality
The space devoted to a topic should be broadly proportional to its significance within the overall Twin. Briefings should neither exaggerate nor minimise observations simply because they are unusual.

Purpose
The Overview Brief is a confidential executive briefing describing the current state of the Digital Career Twin.
Its purpose is to help the user understand every professionally significant characteristic currently represented within the Twin, including recurring patterns, notable observations, uncertainties and areas that may warrant further explanation.
The summary should prioritise completeness and accuracy over brevity.

The Overview Brief should leave a well-informed reader feeling that they understand the structure, trajectory, distinguishing characteristics, and open questions of the career, even if they have not read the underlying evidence.

The Mirror should surface professionally salient observations, regardless of whether they are positive, negative, or neutral.

The Overview Brief is doing three different jobs.
1. Structural observations
Things that are objectively true.
For example:
* Career duration.
* Number of roles.
* Geographic movement.
* Industry changes.
* Promotions.
* Consulting periods.
* Career gaps.
* Concurrent roles.

2. Interpretive observations
Patterns supported by multiple pieces of evidence.
For example:
* Repeated platform transformation.
* Leadership.
* Regulatory environments.
* Cross-functional influence.

3. Attention items
Things a thoughtful recruiter, hiring manager or executive coach would probably ask about.
For example:
* Nine roles in twelve years.
* Nine-month employment gap.
* Transition from permanent employment to consulting.
* Shift into AI.
* Large change in domain.

None of these are criticisms.
They're conversation starters.

Structure

Component	Required?
Career scope (years, roles, geography)	Yes
Career progression	Yes
Strongest recurring patterns	Yes
Distinctive characteristics	If present
Areas of uncertainty	Yes
Confidence statement	Yes
It should have a consistent internal structure.
1. Start with observable facts
The opening paragraph should establish the shape of the career before attempting any interpretation.
For example:
Your Twin currently contains evidence spanning twelve years across nine professional roles in Canada, the UK, the United States and Spain. The evidence shows a progression from engineering and delivery leadership into increasingly strategic technology transformation roles, including software engineering management, programme management, platform engineering and enterprise AI adoption.
Notice that every sentence could be defended by pointing to evidence.

2. Identify the strongest recurring patterns
Only after establishing the facts should it move into synthesis.
For example:
The strongest recurring pattern is leadership of technology transformation. Across multiple organisations, the evidence consistently demonstrates responsibility for organisational change, platform modernisation, operating model design and engineering delivery. Management responsibility is another highly recurrent theme, with repeated leadership of teams, programmes and strategic initiatives.

3. Call out distinctive characteristics
Now answer:
"What makes this career unusual?"
Not every summary needs this section, but if there is something distinctive, surface it.
For example:
The evidence also suggests an unusual combination of strategic and operational work. Many roles combine executive-level planning with hands-on responsibility for implementation, architecture and delivery.

4. Identify uncertainty

Don't say:
"I'm less certain..."
because that anthropomorphises the model.
Instead say:
There is currently less evidence supporting...
or
The available evidence provides less clarity about...
The uncertainty belongs to the evidence, not the AI.

5. End with confidence
Not:
"If this feels right..."
Instead, more like:
This reflection is based on the evidence currently contained within your Twin and will evolve as additional evidence is added or existing evidence is refined.


Writing Perspective
Write as though preparing a confidential briefing for a highly competent executive who wishes to understand every professionally significant characteristic of a person of interest's career.
The tone should be:
* Objective
* Precise
* Evidence-based
* Observational
* Meticulous
* Non-judgemental
The summary should avoid motivational language, coaching language, conversational fillers and unnecessary anthropomorphism.

The Overview Brief should describe the Twin, not narrate the AI's thought process.
For example, avoid:
* I think...
* I feel...
* I'm reading...
* It seems to me...
* My impression is...
* You strike me as...
Prefer:
* The evidence suggests...
* Your Twin currently indicates...
* The strongest evidence supports...
* The available evidence provides...
* There is currently limited evidence for...
Those keep the system grounded.
The Twin doesn't have intuition.
It has evidence.
