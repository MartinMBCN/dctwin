Digital Career Twin Taxonomy

Purpose

The Digital Career Twin is the canonical, persistent semantic model of a person's professional identity.

It consists of concepts rather than documents.

Documents are inputs to, or representations of, the Twin.

Core Concepts
Person

The owner of the Digital Career Twin.

Contains stable factual information and preferences.

Role

A period of professional experience.

A role provides context for evidence.

A role is not evidence.

Evidence

The atomic unit of professional proof.

Evidence represents a claim that can be supported.

Examples include:

achievements
responsibilities
quantified outcomes
stories
projects

Evidence is always associated with a source.

Fact

Information directly stated by the user or extracted with high confidence.

Examples:

company
dates
degree
location

Facts are not inferred.

Inference

A conclusion drawn from evidence.

Examples:

Professional Identity
Career Stage
Theme
Capability

Every inference must contain:

confidence
rationale
supporting evidence
Preference

Information describing desired future state.

Examples:

target industries
preferred locations
compensation
leadership style

Preferences are neither facts nor inferences.

Representation

A context-specific projection of the Digital Career Twin.

Examples:

Career Mirror
CV
Timeline
Company Fit
Interview Brief

Representations are generated.

They are never canonical.

Relationships
Person
    │
    ├── Facts
    ├── Preferences
    ├── Roles
    │       │
    │       └── Evidence
    │
    └── Inferences
Principles
The Twin is canonical.
Evidence is first-class.
Facts and inferences are distinct.
Every inference is explainable.
Representations are projections.
User corrections become evidence.

Twin — the canonical semantic model.
Mirror — the interface through which the user observes and interacts.
Reflection — the current visible state of the Twin.
