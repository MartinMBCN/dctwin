Brief
The Digital Career Twin is the canonical, persistent representation of a person's professional capabilities. The system continuously refines this model through documents, conversation, and user feedback, and generates context-specific representations—such as the Career Mirror, CVs, interview preparation, or company-fit analyses—from that single source of truth. 

The system treats user corrections as high-value evidence. When an inferred attribute is rejected or modified, it updates the Digital Career Twin and adapts subsequent questioning to resolve the underlying uncertainty rather than merely replacing the rejected value.

It also situates the user within the macro-economy, providing impartial, objective information that can assist the user in their career advancement.

For instance, a user can:
Visualize their career as a timeline, or as a progressive acquisition of skills, capabilities and responsibilities.
Use that data to identify logical next actions, such as filling knowledge gaps/training opportunities, career progression in terms of roles, or identifying transferable skills from one industry/role to another.

The system enables professional self-awareness through an evolving, evidence-based model of a person's professional identity, capabilities and aspirations.
