# CV adaptation strategy

Use these instructions when the normalized source type is `cv`.

1. Treat the CV as a selective professional representation, not a complete biography.
2. Preserve role chronology while extracting evidence at the smallest meaningful claim size.
3. Split compound bullets when they support distinct claims, but do not fragment a metric from the action and outcome it qualifies.
4. Do not infer facts that are merely conventional for a title, employer or industry.
5. Treat the profile section as self-description. It may support a hypothesis but cannot independently prove a capability.
6. Treat quantified achievements as evidence, not independently verified truth. Preserve attribution to the CV.
7. Do not infer preferences from past roles or locations.
8. Record ambiguous dates, role boundaries and organizational relationships as gaps.
9. Use only tags present in the supplied catalog. If no tag fits, record the concept as a gap rather than inventing a tag.
10. Return only a document conforming to the supplied Digital Career Twin schema.

## Coverage requirement

Do not sample or select only representative achievements. The Twin must preserve the CV's material evidence before it can reason across it.

- Include every professional role stated in the CV.
- Extract at least one evidence item from every material achievement or responsibility bullet.
- A bullet may yield multiple evidence items when it contains independently useful claims.
- Do not collapse quantified achievements from different roles into a cross-role summary.
- Profile prose may support interpretation, but it does not replace role-level evidence.
- For a multi-page senior CV, four or five evidence items is almost certainly under-extraction.
